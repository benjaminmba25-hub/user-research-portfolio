About Me

I am a User Researcher passionate about creating digital services that work for everyone. My approach is grounded in the Government Design Service (GDS) principles: start with user needs, design for the hardest to reach first, and iterate based on evidence.

I have a particular interest in government digital transformation and designing for vulnerable users—those with limited digital skills, accessibility needs, or challenging life circumstances.

Why Government Digital Services?

Government services affect millions of lives. The scale of impact, the diversity of users, and the mission to make services work for everyone—especially those often overlooked—draws me to this work.

My Research Approach

Aligned with GDS Principles

I follow the Government Design Service approach to user research: start with user needs, design for the hardest to reach first, iterate based on evidence, work in the open, and make things accessible.

Method When I Use It Example Application
User Interviews Discovery phase, understanding needs Speaking to UC claimants about their experience
Ethnography Understanding real-world behaviour Observing how people use Jobcentre services
Usability Testing Evaluating prototypes & services Testing digital forms with screen reader users
